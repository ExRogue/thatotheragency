Website Conversion Checklist for London SMEs

Published 20 February 2026 · 9 min read

Most conversion lifts come from clearer messaging and fewer user-friction points, not expensive rebuilds. This checklist is designed for teams that need practical gains quickly.

1. Make the value proposition obvious in five seconds

Your hero section should say who you help, what result you deliver, and what action to take next.

2. Use one primary CTA per page

Competing calls to action split attention and lower overall conversion probability.

3. Add trust near the decision moment

  • Client logos or named examples
  • Short proof statements
  • Clear delivery process summary

4. Optimise forms for quality and completion

Keep forms concise but include key qualification fields. The objective is useful enquiries, not maximum volume.

5. Review mobile UX thoroughly

Most first visits happen on mobile. Check spacing, input usability, and CTA clarity on smaller screens.

6. Improve speed where it affects conversion

Compress oversized assets and remove heavy scripts from key conversion pages to reduce abandonment.

7. Track conversion stages

Measure more than form submissions: landing views, CTA clicks, form start, form completion, and quality follow-up.

Frequently asked questions

Should we redesign the whole site to improve conversion?

Usually no. Start with high-traffic commercial pages and run focused changes before committing to full redesign work.

How quickly can CRO changes show results?

Simple message and form changes can show impact within weeks when traffic volume is sufficient.
